04 — Image Specs

WHAT TO MAKE & HOW BIG

Every image has exact specs. Follow them precisely — wrong sizes break the layout on mobile.

ImageDimensionsFormatMax SizeNotes
Hero image (Product Spotlight) 600 × 400px JPG or PNG 200KB Dark, moody. Product shot or dramatic box reveal. Dark edges that blend into #1A1A1A background.
Hero image (Promo) 600 × 300px JPG or PNG 200KB Stacked bundle shot or lifestyle group image. Can be brighter.
Product card (Product Grid) 168 × 180px JPG or PNG 80KB each Clean product shot on white or light background. Consistent across all 3.
Founder Note None — — No images needed. Text only.

Retina tip: Design at 2x (e.g. 1200×800 for a 600×400 hero) then export at the dimensions listed above. This keeps images sharp on high-DPI screens.

Image Style Guide

✅ DO
Dark, moody product shots for Product Spotlight
Show puzzles mid-solve (pieces scattered, hands visible)
Lifestyle shots: couples at table, wine, mood lighting
Close-ups of hidden Easter eggs and details
Consistent white/light backgrounds for product cards
Use brand colors in backgrounds and overlays
❌ DON'T
Stock photo energy (generic, staged, corporate)
Bright white flat-lay that looks like every other brand
Kids or family-centric imagery (we're adult-focused)
Text baked into images (keep text in HTML)
Busy backgrounds that compete with the product
Gradients or effects that don't match brand
05 — Rules

DESIGN DO'S & DON'TS

✅ ALWAYS DO
Follow the template layout exactly — don't rearrange sections
Use the exact colors from this brief (#FEE000, not "yellow")
Test on mobile before submitting — everything must work at 320px
Keep CTA buttons full-width on mobile
Use the copy exactly as provided — don't change words
Make the CTA the most visually prominent element
❌ NEVER DO
Add decorative elements not in the template
Use colors not in the brand palette
Change the copy, subject line, or CTA text
Use more than 2 brand colors per email (plus ink + cream)
Make images heavier than the max file sizes listed
Bake text into images — all copy stays in HTML
06 — Process

HOW EMAILS GET MADE

1

Brief + Copy

Willis (AI) writes the campaign brief, copy, subject lines, and image direction. Posted to ClickUp.

2

Design

Martin creates images, builds email in Klaviyo using the template and exact copy provided. Moves task to "for review."

3

Review

Ginny previews in Klaviyo. Approves or requests one round of changes.

4

Schedule

Ginny schedules send in Klaviyo. Task moves to "complete."

Martin's role is visual execution only. You will always receive: the exact copy, which template to use, image direction with references, and CTA text. Your job is to make it look amazing in Klaviyo. Don't change the words. Don't add sections. Just make it beautiful.

07 — Checklist

BEFORE YOU SUBMIT

  • Copy matches the brief EXACTLY — no changes, no typos
  • Correct template used (Product Spotlight / Product Grid / Promo / Founder Note)
  • Brand colors used correctly (check hex codes, not "close enough")
  • All images are correct dimensions and under max file size
  • All links work and go to the right pages
  • CTA button is visible, clickable, and correct text
  • Preview text / preheader is set (not blank)
  • Tested on mobile (Klaviyo preview → mobile view)
  • Subject line entered correctly
  • Unsubscribe link present and working
  • Sent yourself a test email and checked inbox rendering
  • Task moved to "for review" in ClickUp
