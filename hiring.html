Job Description

  • Own and grow revenue across Shopify and Amazon channels
  • Manage Amazon Seller Central — listings, A+ content, PPC, brand registry
  • Shopify storefront optimization — CRO, A/B testing, UX improvements
  • Run and optimize paid campaigns (Amazon Sponsored, Google Shopping)
  • Keyword research and SEO for both platforms
  • Pricing strategy — MAP, bundles, promotions, competitive positioning
  • Product launch execution on each channel
  • Weekly reporting — revenue, CVR, ROAS, traffic, BSR
  • Monitor Amazon account health, policy changes, and compliance
  • Explore new channels (Walmart, TikTok Shop, wholesale)
  • Coordinate with Brand Lead on listing creative
  • Use AI tools for copywriting and data analysis

Interview Questions

"If I said grow our Shopify revenue 30% in 6 months, what's your first move?"
❌ "What's your budget for ads?"
✅ "Let me see your current CVR, AOV, and traffic sources first"
"Our Amazon listing is ranking page 3. Walk me through your approach."
❌ "Increase PPC budget"
✅ "I'd audit the listing copy, images, reviews, then look at keyword indexing before touching ads"
"We sell puzzles on Shopify and Amazon. What's a risk of selling on both?"
❌ "I don't think there's a risk"
✅ "Channel conflict — Amazon can cannibalize DTC margin. Need a strategy for differentiation (bundles, exclusives, pricing)"

90-Day Success Criteria

  • Full audit of Shopify + Amazon performance completed (week 1-2)
  • All listings optimized — copy, images, keywords, A+ content
  • PPC campaigns restructured with clear ROAS targets
  • Weekly reporting dashboard live and shared
  • One product launch executed end-to-end on both channels
  • Channel strategy doc created — pricing, differentiation, roadmap

"We're a puzzle brand. How would you build community around that?"
❌ "Post on social media more"

  • Content calendar live — 30 days planned at all times
  • At least 2 email campaigns sent per week with A/B testing

  • Influencer/UGC pipeline started — 5+ creators identified and contacted

Product Developer
What we make
HIRE 🟣
tap for full details ↓

Job Description

  • Research market trends, competitors, and puzzle industry opportunities
  • Develop new puzzle concepts with Visionary — themes, formats, sizes, difficulty
  • Source and manage manufacturers (primarily China-based)
  • Request, evaluate, and iterate on samples — quality, fit, finish, durability
  • Design and refine packaging — unboxing experience is part of the product
  • Manage BOM (bill of materials) and landed cost per SKU
  • Own the product development calendar — concept to shelf timeline
  • Negotiate pricing, MOQs, and payment terms with factories
  • Ensure compliance — safety certifications, customs, labeling
  • Coordinate with Ops on production schedules and inventory planning
  • Coordinate with Brand Lead on product photography and lifestyle imagery
  • Track competitor product launches and market gaps

Budget (USD)

Global Remote
$1,500–3,500/mo
North America
$4,000–6,000/mo
Vancouver Local
$5,000–7,500/mo
China-Based
$1,500–3,000/mo

Where to Find

Upwork — "product development sourcing" LinkedIn — CPG / consumer products Emily Carr / design school grads (Vancouver) Alibaba sourcing agent networks Puzzle / board game industry contacts Canton Fair connections
  • China-based sourcing agent with product dev skills can be incredible value
  • Look for physical product experience — not just digital
  • Puzzle/game/toy industry experience is a huge plus
  • Must understand manufacturing constraints, not just design

Interview Questions

"If I told you to launch a new puzzle SKU, what's the first thing you do?"
❌ "What do you want it to look like?"
✅ "What's the margin target? Who's the customer? What's selling well now? What gaps exist in the lineup?"
"A factory sends you a sample and the quality isn't right. What do you do?"
❌ "Tell them to redo it"
✅ "Document specific issues with photos, reference the spec sheet, get on a call to align expectations, request revised sample with timeline"
"How do you decide between two factories — one cheaper, one better quality?"
❌ Goes with cheaper immediately
✅ "Depends on the product positioning. For a premium brand, quality is non-negotiable. I'd calculate total landed cost including defect rates, returns, and brand impact"

90-Day Success Criteria

  • Full product line audit — margins, performance, gaps identified
  • Manufacturer relationship map documented (current + backup factories)
  • Next 2-3 SKU concepts developed with cost estimates
  • Product development calendar created — 12-month pipeline view
  • At least 1 new product in sample stage
  • BOM and landed cost template standardized for all SKUs

Finance
The numbers
HIRE 🔵
tap for full details ↓

Job Description

  • Manage bookkeeping — accounts payable, accounts receivable, reconciliations
  • Monthly P&L, balance sheet, and cash flow statements
  • Budget creation and variance tracking
  • Track profitability by channel (Shopify, Amazon, Kickstarter)
  • Track profitability by SKU — identify winners and losers
  • Manage payroll, contractor payments, and tax filings
  • Cash flow forecasting — especially around large inventory buys
  • Support Product Dev with cost analysis and margin modeling
  • Sales tax / GST / HST compliance across jurisdictions
  • Amazon fee reconciliation and reimbursement claims
  • Financial planning for Kickstarter campaigns and product launches
  • Work directly with Visionary on strategic financial decisions

Budget (USD)

Global Remote
$1,000–2,500/mo
North America
$3,000–5,000/mo
Vancouver Bookkeeper
$2,500–4,000/mo
Fractional CFO
$1,500–3,000/mo

Where to Find

Upwork — "ecommerce bookkeeper" Belay (fractional finance) LinkedIn — "ecomm controller" Local CPA firms (Vancouver) A2X / Xero partner network
  • Best value: Pakistan, Philippines, Eastern Europe for bookkeeping
  • MUST have ecommerce experience — Amazon fees and multi-channel reconciliation is complex
  • Consider splitting: remote bookkeeper + fractional CFO for strategy
  • Familiarity with Xero/QuickBooks + A2X (Amazon accounting) is key

Interview Questions

"We sell on Shopify and Amazon. How do you track profitability by channel?"
❌ "I just look at the revenue numbers"
✅ "Revenue minus COGS, platform fees, shipping, ads, and returns per channel. Amazon has hidden fees — referral, FBA, storage. I'd use A2X to automate the reconciliation"
"We need to place a $50K inventory order. How do you help me decide?"
❌ "Do you have the cash?"
✅ "I'd model cash flow impact — when does the cash go out vs. when do we see revenue? What's our runway after? Can we negotiate payment terms? What's the expected ROI by SKU?"

90-Day Success Criteria

  • Books cleaned up and current — all reconciliations done
  • Monthly financial reporting cadence established (by 10th of each month)
  • Profitability by channel and by top SKUs calculated
  • Cash flow forecast model built (12-month rolling)
  • Amazon fee audit completed — reimbursement claims filed
  • Budget template created for next quarter

Customer Service
How we care
HIRE 🟠

Job Description

  • Respond to customer inquiries across all channels (email, chat, Amazon, social DMs)
  • Handle returns, exchanges, refunds, and replacements
  • Manage Amazon buyer messages within 24hr SLA
  • Resolve product issues — missing pieces, damaged items, defects
  • Proactively request and manage product reviews
  • Escalate product quality issues to Product Dev
  • Build and maintain FAQ, help center, and response templates
  • Monitor and respond to negative reviews (Amazon + Shopify)
  • Track common complaints and surface patterns to team
  • Post-purchase follow-up — satisfaction checks, review requests
  • Use AI tools for templated responses and ticket triage
  • Turn unhappy customers into fans — go above and beyond on resolution

Budget (USD)

Global Remote
$800–1,500/mo
Philippines
$800–1,200/mo
North America
$2,500–4,000/mo
Part-Time / VA
$500–1,000/mo

Where to Find

Upwork OnlineJobs.ph LinkedIn Gorgias / Zendesk partner networks
  • Philippines is strong for CS — English fluency, service culture, good timezone overlap
  • Start part-time and scale with volume
  • Amazon CS experience is specifically valuable — buyer messages have strict SLAs
  • Look for empathy + problem-solving, not just "fast typing"

Interview Questions

"A customer says their puzzle is missing 3 pieces and leaves a 1-star review. What do you do?"
❌ "Send a replacement"
✅ "Apologize immediately, offer replacement + small gift. Then contact them privately to ask if they'd update the review. Flag the issue to Product Dev to check the batch"
"You notice the same complaint coming up 5 times this week. What's your move?"
❌ "Keep resolving each ticket"
✅ "Document the pattern, alert the team with data, suggest a fix — maybe it's a listing issue, packaging problem, or manufacturing defect that needs escalation"

90-Day Success Criteria

  • All response channels set up and monitored daily
  • Average response time ≤ 12 hours
  • Template library created for top 20 inquiry types
  • Review request system live and running
  • Monthly CS report — volume, themes, CSAT, resolution rate
  • Escalation process documented (when to flag Product Dev, Ops, etc.)
