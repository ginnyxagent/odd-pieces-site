Campaign Status Recipients
Total number of people the email was sent to.
 Open RateTarget ≥ 40%
% of recipients who opened the email. Low = subject line is weak or email is landing in spam. Fix: A/B test 3 subject lines every send.
 CTORTarget ≥ 10%
Click-to-Open Rate — of people who OPENED, how many clicked? Better than raw click rate because it measures content quality, not just reach. Low CTOR = the email content isn't compelling enough. Fix: stronger hook, single CTA, less clutter.
 Click RateTarget ≥ 2.5%
% of ALL recipients who clicked. Affected by both subject line (opens) and content quality (CTOR). Use alongside CTOR for full picture.
 Rev / EmailHigher = better
Revenue generated divided by emails sent. The north star metric — every decision should move this number up. Typical range: $0.05–$0.30+ per email.
 UnsubTarget ≤ 0.2%
% who unsubscribed after receiving this email. High unsub = content is irrelevant or sending too frequently. Damages deliverability over time.

Flows are automated sequences triggered by behaviour (signup, purchase, cart abandon). They run 24/7 and should generate 30–50% of total email revenue. Drafts are turned off — they're making $0.

Flow Status
Live = running and sending automatically.
Draft = turned OFF — earning $0. These need to be activated.
Manual = requires manual trigger.
 Recipients
Total people who entered this flow in the last 12 months.
 Open RateTarget ≥ 40%
Flows should have HIGHER open rates than campaigns because they're triggered by behaviour (high intent moments). Low = email timing or subject line is off.
 CTORTarget ≥ 10%
Click-to-Open Rate — of people who opened, how many clicked? Flows should outperform campaigns here because recipients just did something (bought, abandoned cart, etc). Low = content doesn't match their intent.
 Rev / RecipientHigher = better
Revenue generated per person who entered this flow. Flows should generate 3–5× more than campaigns per recipient. Key metric for prioritising which flows to improve first.
 Total RevenueLast 12 months
Total revenue this flow has generated in the past year. Use this to prioritise — fix high-traffic low-revenue flows first.
 Health
Quick flag based on open rate, CTOR, and revenue per recipient. Draft flows are always flagged — they are off and earning nothing.
