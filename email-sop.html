🧩 Odd Pieces — Email Agency SOP

Effective immediately · Last updated March 2025

This document replaces the current approval-based workflow. Every email campaign follows this process — no exceptions.

You do not pitch us ideas. We give you the brief. You execute.

01 Campaign Brief

Every campaign begins with a completed Campaign Brief from Odd Pieces. No brief = no work starts.

The brief covers: send date, segment, goal, hook, key message, CTA, tone, and assets. Wait for it before touching anything.

02 Copy First

Submit copy only — no design until copy is approved. Every submission must include:

  • Subject line — 3 options minimum
  • Preview text
  • Body copy (if it takes more than 30 seconds to read, it's too long)
  • CTA — one clear action, not multiple
Copy standards
  • Every email has ONE goal. One hook. One CTA.
  • Use consumer psychology: scarcity, social proof, curiosity gaps, loss aversion — whatever fits the send
  • No filler. No "we're excited to share..." No generic openers.
  • Write like a person, not a brand bot.

03 Design

Once copy is approved, design follows the copy — it supports the message, it doesn't decorate around it.

Design standards
  • Visual hierarchy must guide the eye toward the CTA
  • No decorative elements that don't serve the communication
  • Mobile-first — if it doesn't work on phone, it doesn't work
  • Every image earns its place: context, emotion, or urgency

04 Revisions

One revision round per campaign. Come back strong. If it still misses after revisions, we talk about why.

KPIs — You Own These

These are your numbers. We review monthly.

Open rate≥ 40%

Monthly report due: First Monday of each month, covering the prior month. Include: what worked, what didn't, what changes next month.

Miss targets two months in a row → we have a performance conversation.

What "Good" Looks Like

Before submitting anything, ask yourself:

  • Does this sound like Odd Pieces?
  • Is there ONE clear reason to click?
  • Is there urgency or a reason to act now?
  • Would a 35-year-old creative professional forward this to a friend?

If the answer to any of these is no — revise before submitting.
