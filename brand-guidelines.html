01 — Who We Are

The Brand Story

Odd Pieces started with a simple, slightly unhinged idea: what if a puzzle was actually a story? Not a pretty landscape you assemble and forget. A story you live inside.

We make 1000-piece jigsaw puzzles for people who hate boring things. Every Odd Pieces puzzle has a twist — a before and after hidden inside the same image — and a Secret Ending Envelope that gets opened only once you're done. The reveal is the whole point.


We're not a faceless puzzle company. We're a small, passionate team who genuinely believes a cardboard box can make two adults put their phones down, open a bottle of wine, and lose 4 hours they'll never regret. That's the whole mission.


Our puzzles exist in a connected universe. Hidden Easter eggs in every title reference the others. Characters reappear. There's a metanarrative if you're paying attention. Most people aren't. That's fine — the payoff is even bigger when they finally notice.

"The gift that surprises everyone — even the person who has everything."

Reference Brands Areaware's aesthetic sensibility (design-store premium, indie cool, slightly offbeat) + Exploding Kittens' wit (irreverent, genius, fun). NOT Magic Puzzle Company. NOT a generic puzzle brand.
02 — How It Works

The 3-Step Experience

Every Odd Pieces puzzle is a three-act story. This is the product experience — the thing that makes people buy it, gift it, and post about it at 1am.

Step Inside a Story

The box cover is a dense Wimmelbilder illustration — Where's Waldo-level density, micro-stories everywhere. A before comic inside sets the scene. You know what's happening. For now.

Build What Happens After

The puzzle you actually assemble is the same scene, 15 seconds later. Hundreds of changes. You discover them piece by piece. The twist is what makes people gasp, laugh, and point across the table.

Unlock the Secret Ending

A sealed SECRET ENDING ENVELOPE is waiting inside. Open it only after completing the puzzle. Inside: the after comic revealing the shocking why + a final riddle. The grand reveal.

You'll find things in here you weren't supposed to find.

04 — Who It's For

Our Audience

Not your grandma's puzzle. (Unless she's cool.) Odd Pieces is for adults who want something genuinely fun to do at home — mostly with their partner, sometimes solo, occasionally with the whole crew.

Primary Buyer

Adults 30–45 who are done with Netflix and need something to do with their hands. High income, has taste, wants fun that doesn't feel childish. Most do it with their partner over wine. FUNDULT / KIDULT energy. Not everyone has kids — and that's the point.

The Table

Adults who want a challenge. Solo puzzlers who put on a podcast and disappear for 4 hours. Couples who need a reason to put their phones down. The kind of person who reads the instruction manual because they actually enjoy it.

The Gift Person

Gifting is a bonus, not the pitch. But when someone does gift it — the reaction is always "HOW DID YOU FIND THIS." Not another candle. Not another generic game. Something that makes people go wait... what is this?

The Keyword That Drives Everything

Fundult / Kidult

Grown adults who still love fun. Who haven't decided that joy is something you age out of. Who want something clever, surprising, and worth doing. Our entire brand voice is written for them.

Voice Principles

Shock First, Explain Later

Lead with the surprising, weird, funny thing. Let curiosity do the work. Don't front-load context.

Confident, Not Arrogant

We know we're different. We don't need to yell about it. Confidence is quiet. Arrogance is loud.

Play It Straight

The best humor is delivered deadpan. Don't wink too hard. Say the absurd thing like it's obvious.

  • Ink outline: #1A1A1A — clean black, no softening
  • Light blue circle: #C1E3E8 — bottom of the stem. The detail that rewards close looking.

09 — Typography

Type System

Two fonts. No exceptions. Nunito owns the personality. Inter handles the rest. Together they're punchy, clean, and unmistakably Odd Pieces.

Nunito · Google Fonts · Display / Headlines / UI Labels

Nunito 900

Headline 800

Subheader 700

Section Tag / Label · 11px · 800 · +0.18em tracking

Inter · Google Fonts · Body / Functional Copy

Inter Regular 400 — body copy, descriptions, longer reads. This is where the voice of the brand lives in paragraph form. Keep it warm, direct, and never boring.

Inter Medium 500 — callouts, emphasized body, UI text.

Inter SemiBold 600 — small labels, metadata, button text.

Type Rules — Non-Negotiable

No Outlined Text

No text with outlined/hollow strokes. Flat filled letterforms only.

No Drop Shadows on Type

No CSS text-shadow or Illustrator drop shadows on any letterforms.

No Gradients on Type

No gradient fills on letterforms. Flat color only. Always.

10 — Art Direction

Illustration Direction

Each puzzle has its own illustration style. That's intentional. What's consistent isn't the style — it's the philosophy. Maximum density. Hidden stories. Comedic payoffs. Wimmelbilder soul.

🔍 Maximum Density

Every inch of the illustration is packed with micro-stories. A character in the bottom-left corner has their own subplot. Find it if you can. Wimmelbilder — Where's Waldo-level layering.

🎭 Comedic Payoffs

Humor lives in the details. The thing you spot after three hours of puzzling. The absurdist twist you weren't expecting. Never mean-spirited. Always landing.

🎮 Pop Culture References

Layered, witty, earned. Not forced. Think: something that makes one person at the table gasp and the others feel like they missed a joke they'd love once explained.

🪄 Before → After

Every illustration must work as a before scene AND imply the chaos that follows. The box cover is the before. The puzzle is the after. They must be legible as a pair.

🎨 Style Varies Per Title

Pixel art for one puzzle. Clean vector isometric for another. Painterly for the next. The brand doesn't lock one illustration style — each title can look distinct while sharing the Odd Pieces philosophy.

🧵 Metauniverse Threads

Every illustration must hide at least one Easter egg referencing another Odd Pieces title. A character, an object, a logo, a detail. The universe is connected. Show it.

On AI vs. Human Artists Odd Pieces works with real artists from around the world. Not AI-generated art. This is brand gospel — the human authorship behind each illustration is part of the product's soul and a key differentiator in how we talk about the work.

Humor Tone for Illustrations

Absurdist ✓

Situations that escalate beyond reason. Vacuum cleaner destroys a city. Mushrooms go full chaos. The premise is weird and the execution commits.

Irreverent ✓

Characters that seem to know they're in a puzzle. Winking references. Meta moments that reward the attentive.

Never Mean ✗

Humor punches up or punches sideways. Never down. The comedy is about situations, not mocking people.

11 — Product

Packaging

The unboxing is the first touchpoint. It should feel like an Apple box crossed with a comic book. Premium cardboard. Tight, satisfying, slow-open. Every insert earns its place.

📦

The Box

Premium cardboard. Tight lid. Slow, satisfying open. The weight is right. The cover illustration is the before scene — dense, full-bleed, no dead space.

📜

Before Comic

Sets the scene. You know what's happening before you start. Makes the twist land harder when you discover what changed.

🧩

1000 Puzzle Pieces

Premium cut. The assembled image is the after scene — same frame, 15 seconds later. Hundreds of differences. You find them as you go.

🔒

Secret Ending Envelope

Sealed. "Open after completing." Contains the after comic + final riddle. This is the product's climax. Don't open early. The envelope design should feel consequential.

⚠️

Warning Insert Card

Signature brand moment. "This puzzle contains things you weren't supposed to find." Sets tone. Primes the hunt. Should feel like a prop from the puzzle universe.

🌟

Sticker Sheet

Extra delighter. Branded, in-world, collectible-feeling. Characters from the puzzle universe. Something people actually use.

Box Design Principles

  • → Full-bleed illustration — no white borders or obvious branding frames
  • → Title treatment in Nunito 900, uppercase, integrated into the illustration
  • → Small Odd Pieces logo emblem, not competing with the art
  • → "1000 PIECES" callout — visible, confident, not apologetic

The Apple Comparison

The unboxing should feel premium before the puzzle does. The lid should have resistance — that satisfying slow peel. Every insert should feel intentional and placed. Nothing rattles. Nothing feels like it was an afterthought. First impression = lasting impression.

12 — Distribution

Channel Guidance

Each channel has its own rules. Same brand, different contexts. Know which game you're playing.

🛍️

Shopify — oddpieces.com

Lead with blue and yellow — it's the brand's home, full expression
Hero section: the 3-step experience front and center
Product pages: illustration detail shots that reward close inspection
Tone: full brand voice, unfiltered
Show the metauniverse — cross-sell is lore, not just sales
The Secret Ending Envelope should be teased, never spoiled
📦

Amazon

Main image: puzzle box on white background — clean, no clutter
Secondary images: illustration close-ups, lifestyle in-play shots
A+ content: tell the 3-step story with imagery
7-image sequence: box → illustration detail → unboxing → in-play → Secret Envelope → after reveal → full family shot
Title: lead with the puzzle name, then "1000-Piece Jigsaw Puzzle"
Bullets: experience-first. Not "300gsm premium board."
K

Kickstarter

Campaign page should feel like a manifesto, not a product page
Lead with the "WAIT — WHAT?!" energy upfront
Backer tiers: name them like they're part of the universe (not "Early Bird Tier 2")
Updates: talk to backers like insiders, not customers
Show the team. Show the process. Human brand, all the way.
Stretch goals = puzzle universe expansion moments
B&N

Barnes & Noble — Retail

Shelf rule: make someone pick it up from 3 feet away
Box cover must read at a distance — bold, dense, visually arresting
Title treatment: large, uppercase, Nunito 900 — no elegance, just presence
Side panel: "NOT YOUR ORDINARY PUZZLES" — hook before they even flip it
Back panel: the 3-step experience, scannable bullets, the reveal tease
No price war positioning — shelf presence is about being unmissable
13 — What Makes Us Different

The Human Brand

This is the stuff that can't be faked. The things that separate Odd Pieces from every puzzle company that's just printing cardboard. These aren't features — they're personality.

✉️

They Mail Puzzle Pieces

If a customer emails about a missing piece, someone on the team literally grabs a puzzle piece and mails it to them. Not a form. Not a replacement box. An actual puzzle piece in an actual envelope. This is the brand in action.

🤫

Clues, Not Answers

When someone emails asking for the answer to the final riddle, they get clues — not the solution. The experience is sacred. Spoiling it would be a betrayal. The team actively protects the mystery, even when customers beg.

🎨

Real Artists, Real People

Every illustration is made by a real human artist from somewhere in the world. Not AI-generated. Not templated. The artists are credited, supported, and part of the brand story. When you buy Odd Pieces, you're also buying someone's work.

🏃

Small, Passionate Team

Not a faceless corporation. A small team that genuinely believes in what they're building. This comes through in everything — the copy, the packaging details, the customer service. The brand has a pulse because the team does.

The Rule Every brand decision should feel like it was made by a human for a human. If it feels automated, templated, or corporate — it's wrong. The human brand is not a marketing strategy. It's who Odd Pieces actually is.
14 — Rules

Do / Don't

The short version of everything above. Print this out. Put it on the wall. Check every design against it.

✓ Do
Use Odd Yellow (#FEE000) and Blue (#C1E3E8) heavily — both are hero colors
Use Nunito 900 uppercase for all headlines and display text
Lead with the twist, the shock, the "WAIT — WHAT?!" moment
Make the document / design itself feel fun and energetic
Let illustration styles vary per title — the philosophy is what's consistent
Credit and celebrate the artists behind each illustration
Keep the Secret Ending sacred — tease, never spoil
Write copy that sounds like a smart, irreverent human
Use the Wimmelbilder philosophy — maximum density, everywhere
Hide metauniverse Easter eggs in every illustration
Make the flat logo system — no stacked layers, no outlines
The small light blue circle at the bottom of the P — always there
✗ Don't
Add drop shadows, gradients, or outlines to typography
Use the stacked-offset multi-copy logo (that's the old version)
Sound like Magic Puzzle Company — differentiate actively
Use luxury editorial tone ("an immersive puzzle experience")
Sound like a children's activity brand (too soft, too safe)
Use AI-generated art for puzzle illustrations
Spoil the Secret Ending in any marketing material
Automate customer service in ways that feel robotic
Let any channel feel generic, corporate, or templated
Use only yellow and ignore blue (both are dominant)
Position as "for kids" OR "adults only" — it's both, always
Create new puzzle titles that don't connect to the metauniverse
